Mei koeng
Artocarpus altilis x A. mariannensis   

Mei koeng whole fruit

Photo © Jim Wiseman
 
 
 
Geographic Origin of Accession: Federated States of Micronesia
Locality: Chuuk: Losap
Other Names: Mwe serang, Weang, Te mai keang
Notes: Name means "hand" or "branched like coral" referring to the serrated, deeply dissected leaves. Also called Mwe serang, or Weang. Te mai keang in Gilberts. A common atoll variety. Seeded varieties are known as "Meias" with two types: deeply incised leaves (Mei koeng) or entire (Mei chocho). There may be 1-2 types in these categories. Both are widely grown in the atolls of Chuuk, but are not found in the Lagoon islands. Trees are massive specimens often growing 5m before branching with huge trunks and buttress roots, to heights of 20 m or taller.
