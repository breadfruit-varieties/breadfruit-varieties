Ma'afala
Artocarpus altilis (Parkinson) Fosberg

Geographic Origin of Accession: Samoa
Locality: Upolu
Distribution: Samoa, Tonga, Fiji, Niue, Cook Islands, Tuvalu, Vanuatu, Micronesia, Australia, southern Florida
Notes: Tends to be a shorter, more compact tree than most varieties. Small-fruited. The creamy to pale yellow flesh is not as dense and firm as typical Polynesian varieties, with a tender texture when cooked. This variety has been successfully propagated using micro-propagation and plants can be purchased from Cultivaris (http://www.globalbreadfruit.com).
Season: July - December, some off-season fruiting January-May over a 10 year study period.
